Date of Project: January 2025 - August 2025

Project Role: Team President

Project Description

Partnering with Shield-X Technologies, our team developed Qualitex, a cost-effective and scalable system designed to improve textile quality testing for personal protective equipment. The system utilizes a vibration platform and a suite of precision sensors to excite and measure fabric samples from multiple directions. Using our proprietary software, the collected data is processed to extract key material characteristics such as damping coefficient and damping ratio, enabling data-driven material selection for enhanced safety and performance.
As team lead, I managed communication with our spoonsored company, oversaw the project timeline, and led the mechanical design effort. I also gained hands-on experience in precision machining, extrusion frame design, 3D printing for functional components, custom PCB development, and electrical integration. The project successfully delivered a fully functional prototype within the original schedule and was transitioned to Shield-X for pilot testing.

Date of Project: January 2024 ‚Äì April 2024

The objective of this project was to develop a robust, small-scale industrial automation system using LabVIEW and a National Instruments USB-6501 Data Acquisition (DAQ) device. The system controlled a Bytronic ICT3 (Industrial Control Technology) trainer designed to simulate an automated manufacturing line, featuring component sorting, part assembly, and inspection stages using sensors and actuators.
My LabVIEW program was designed to identify and sort aluminum pegs and plastic hoops introduced at random, assemble them together, and inspect each completed unit for accuracy. Through this project, I gained hands-on experience in automation logic design, industrial control hardware integration, and the fundamentals of closed-loop process automation.

Quick Release Steering Wheel Design

(Team Phantom Entrance Project)

Date of Project: May 2022 ‚Äì August 2022

Project Role: Mechanical Designer

Project Description

This project was developed as an entrance submission for SFU Team Phantom Formula SAE Electric. The objective was to design a quick release steering wheel system suitable for use in high-performance racing environments, where rapid driver egress and compact cockpit design are critical.

The system was composed of four main parts: the hub, quick release base, quick release mechanism with latch, and steering wheel. The design featured a 7-bolt hub-to-base connection, a 10-pin bearing-style quick release for rotational stability, and a smoothed compact wheel profile for improved ergonomics, reduced material use, and increased driver legroom.

My contributions included developing initial concept sketches, performing full 3D CAD modeling and structural analysis in SolidWorks, and evaluating material selections such as aluminum, stainless steel, and composites. Static simulations tested torque loads up to 750 Nm and pull forces of 450 N, validating the structural integrity of the connection assembly. The project demonstrated both mechanical design proficiency and an understanding of racing ergonomics, earning me acceptance into Team Phantom‚Äôs Frame and Aero Division.

Technologies Used

  • SolidWorks 3D Modeling and Finite Element Analysis (FEA)
  • Material Research and Selection (Aluminum, Stainless Steel, Composites)
  • Concept Sketching and Design Documentation
  • Torque and Tensile Load Simulation

Project Outcomes

  • Designed a complete quick release steering system meeting FSAE safety and ergonomic standards.
  • Validated design under 750 Nm torque and 450 N tensile loads.
  • Earned entry into SFU Team Phantom Formula SAE Electric as a Frame and Aero team member.
  • Improved understanding of design-for-safety and driver ergonomics in compact racing environments.
