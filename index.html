About

I am Adriana Lee, a Mechatronic Systems Engineering graduate from Simon Fraser University with a strong interest in mechanical design, robotics, and system integration. I am passionate about developing innovative and sustainable engineering solutions that combine mechanical, electrical, and software disciplines to solve real-world problems.
During my time at Powertech Labs, I worked as part of the engineering team supporting hydrogen infrastructure development. I designed and implemented mechanical testing systems, including a custom hydrogen particulate test that was successfully integrated into company testing protocols. I also designed the mechanical component of an automated hydrogen test bench and developed the company’s SolidWorks and AutoCAD component libraries, streamlining design workflows across multiple teams. This role strengthened my expertise in 3D modeling, design documentation, and prototype development, while teaching me to collaborate effectively across electrical, software, and manufacturing disciplines.
I have also led and contributed to award-winning design projects, including an electronic Braille tablet and automated assembly prototypes, which challenged me to think critically, iterate quickly, and deliver practical solutions under tight timelines.
As a Project Lead for Team Phantom, SFU’s Formula SAE electric race car team, I designed and manufactured precision jigs and mounting systems to support the assembly of the vehicle’s frame and subsystems. This experience deepened my understanding of collaborative design, precision manufacturing, and mechanical performance under real-world constraints while strengthening my leadership and project management skills.
I am constantly seeking new skills to learn and technologies to master, and I am motivated by projects that blend sustainability, innovation, and hands-on design. My goal is to continue developing as an engineer through work that pushes boundaries, encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, and makes a meaningful impact.

Qualitex – Automated Fabric Testing

(Capstone Project)

Date of Project: January 2025 - August 2025

Project Role: Team President

Project Description

Partnering with Shield-X Technologies, our team developed Qualitex, a cost-effective and scalable system designed to improve textile quality testing for personal protective equipment. The system utilizes a vibration platform and a suite of precision sensors to excite and measure fabric samples from multiple directions. Using our proprietary software, the collected data is processed to extract key material characteristics such as damping coefficient and damping ratio, enabling data-driven material selection for enhanced safety and performance.
As team lead, I managed communication with our spoonsored company, oversaw the project timeline, and led the mechanical design effort. I also gained hands-on experience in precision machining, extrusion frame design, 3D printing for functional components, custom PCB development, and electrical integration. The project successfully delivered a fully functional prototype within the original schedule and was transitioned to Shield-X for pilot testing.

Technologies Used

  • SolidWorks CAD Modelling and Simulation
  • Custom PCB Design
  • MATLAB for Data Analysis, System Calibration, and User Interface
  • Arduino, Accelerometers, and Hall Effect Sensors for Data Acquisition
  • 3D Printing for Functional Components and Rapid Prototyping
  • Precision Machining including Vertical Saws, Drills, Mills, and Various Hand Tools

Project Outcomes

  • Designed a fully integrated, automated fabric testing platform.
  • Delivered a working prototype on schedule to Shield-X Technologies.
  • Demonstrated reliable data capture and material comparion capability.
  • Showcased at SFU's Mechatronic Capstone Exhibition

Powertech Labs Hydrogen Stations Team Projects

Image from Powertech Labs Inc. Linkedin

Date of Project: September 2024 - August 2025

Project Role: Manufacturing Co Op Student / CADD Technician

Project 1: Hydrogen Station Automated Test Bench

Designed the mechanical components for an automated test bench aimed at enabling safe, efficient, and after-hours testing of high-pressure hydrogen fueling stations. System elements included high pressure piping, extrusion framing, valves, pipe connections, gauges, and other gas elements. My contribution took the mechanical system from initial P&ID concept to a fully realized 3D design, including equipment procurement, specification of existing resources, and performing detailed cost analysis to ensure feasibility and efficiency. The system integrates structural supports, sensor instrumentation, and safety considerations to allow reliable testing without operator risk.

Technologies Used
  • SoldiWorks CAD Modelling
  • P&ID Interpretation
  • Cost Analysis and Equipment Specification
  • Mechanical Assembly Planning
Outcomes
  • Delivered complete mechanical design ready for fabrication.
  • Optimized budget and resource usage.
Project 2: CAD Library Development

Compiled and standardized 3D and 2D files of commonly used components in PTI hydrogen station designs, consolidating them into easy-to-use libraries with SolidWorks PDM and AutoCAD Tool Palettes. By the end of my contract, I had incorporated over 500 parts into the library, creating a comprehensive resource that streamlined workflows and reduced project turnaround times.

Technologies Used
  • SolidWorks / SolidWorks PDM
  • AutoCAD
  • CAD Library Structuring and Standardization
  • Supplier Communication
Outcomes
  • Streamlined engineering and design workflows.
  • Enabled rapid prototyping for multiple hydrogen station designs.
  • Reduced errors and duplication in project documentation.
  • Created a long-term resource for the engineering team.
Project 3: Hydrogen Particulate Test Jig

Designed and fabricated a hydrogen particulate testing system to ensure hydrogen leaving fueling stations is free of contaminants. The project aimed to standardize and document particulate testing procedures, incorporate safety components in all future tests, and maintain sterile components prior to each test, contributing to more accurate and reliable results. Under supervision, I brought the project from concept to creation, including P&ID design, component procurement, 3D modeling, construction assistance, and project documentation.

Technologies Used
  • Solidworks CAD Modelling
  • Mechanical Fabrication Tools
  • P&ID Development
  • Procedural Documentation
Outcomes
  • Verified hydrogen purity for station safety.
  • Standardized testing procedure for reproducible results.
  • Added safety features for all future testing.
  • Contributed to documentation and regulatory compliance.
Project 4: NCR Reporting System

Developed a Non-Conformance Reporting (NCR) System to track components failing to meet specifications in alignment with TSBC standards. The system streamlined reporting, improved accountability, and ensured manufacturing quality.

Technologies Used
  • Microsoft Excel / SharePoint
  • Documentation & Reporting Workflows
Outcomes
  • Ensured regulatory compliance with TSBC.
  • Reduced manufacturing errors.
  • Improved traceability of non-conforming components.

NSP – Hydrogen System Design

Image from Integrated Environmental Solutions

Date of Project: January 2023 – August 2023

Project Role: Junior Mechanical Designer

Project Description

As part of the mechanical design team at Smith + Andersen, I contributed to the mechanical systems design for the New St. Paul’s Hospital — one of British Columbia’s largest healthcare infrastructure projects. My work focused on HVAC layout coordination, medical gas system routing, and ensuring design compliance with hospital and code standards. I collaborated closely with electrical and structural teams to resolve interdisciplinary clashes, developed coordinated Revit models, and produced detailed construction drawings. This project gave me experience with large-scale healthcare design, BIM coordination, and applying mechanical design principles to highly regulated environments.

Technologies Used

  • Autodesk Revit for 3D BIM Modelling and Coordination
  • AutoCAD for Construction Drawings and Detailing
  • Mechanical System Design for Healthcare Facilities (HVAC, Plumbing, Medical Gas)
  • ASHRAE and CSA Standards for Hospital Design

Project Outcomes

  • Supported mechanical system design for a multi-building, large-scale hospital project.
  • Produced coordinated Revit and AutoCAD models for HVAC and medical gas layouts.
  • Contributed to resolving mechanical–electrical–structural coordination issues.
  • Gained in-depth experience with healthcare design standards and BIM workflows.
